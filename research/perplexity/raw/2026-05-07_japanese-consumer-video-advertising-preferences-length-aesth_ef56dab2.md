#### (6) Cultural Context: Why These Preferences Exist
Japan's preferences stem from cultural norms emphasizing subtlety, quality, and social harmony, contrasting US/global hype-driven ads:
- **Kuuki yomi (reading the air)**: Consumers avoid overt sales pressure; "calm, trust-first" creatives ([3]) align with intuitive, non-disruptive communication to avoid social friction.
- **Monozukuri aesthetics (craftsmanship)**: "Show the process/system" videos ([3]) reflect appreciation for meticulous detail and authenticity, prioritizing polished-yet-genuine demos over flashy UGC or disjointed hype.
- **Tatemae (public face/harmony)**: Ads must maintain group-oriented politeness—slow/narrative pacing builds subtle trust vs. fast/disjointed formats that feel intrusive. This drives preference for subtitles, in-stream integration, and local platforms ([1-3]), as Japan's "privacy-conscious" users ([7]) favor non-aggressive, contextual experiences amid aging demographics and LINE/Yahoo dominance.
