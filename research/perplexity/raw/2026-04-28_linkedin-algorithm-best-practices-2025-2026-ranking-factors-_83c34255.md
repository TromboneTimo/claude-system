## Algorithm Signals and Ranking Factors

**Dwell time** is now the dominant engagement metric, vastly outweighing likes.[1] Posts with 61+ seconds of dwell time achieve 15.6% engagement compared to 1.2% for posts with 0-3 seconds of dwell time.[1] The algorithm detects "click bounces" where users click but leave immediately, and deprioritizes such content.[3]

**Comments** carry **15x the algorithmic weight** of likes, and comment threads trigger aggressive reach expansion.[1] However, the search results do not provide specific data on comment depth ratios or share-to-like metrics.

LinkedIn replaced its entire ranking infrastructure in late 2025 with **360Brew**, a unified AI system with 150 billion parameters trained on LinkedIn's data.[2] This system prioritizes "Depth and Authority" over viral reach and now evaluates your profile as a credibility signal before distributing your content.[2][3] Your LinkedIn headline, About section, and experience directly influence post distribution—vague profiles reduce reach even with excellent content.[1]

**External links receive a 60% reach penalty** compared to identical posts without links, and the "link in first comment" workaround is now also penalized as of early 2026.[3]

The algorithm has shifted from a **Social Graph** (distribution based on connections) to an **Interest Graph** (distribution based on content relevance to user interests), explaining the 63-66% impression decline since 2023 while engagement per post increased 12-39%.[1]

## Post Format Performance Data

**Document posts (PDF carousels)** achieve the highest engagement rates at **6.6% average engagement**, with some engaged niches reaching 40%+.[3] This is the top-performing format in 2026.

**Native video** is the second-highest performer, growing **36% year-over-year** and 2x faster than other formats, with optimal length between 30-90 seconds.[3][4]

**Standard text posts struggle to break 2% engagement**, representing a significant decline in performance.[3]

The search results do not provide specific performance metrics for single images, polls, articles, newsletters, or detailed breakdowns of other formats. Newsletters reportedly "bypass the feed entirely," but engagement data is not provided.[3]

## Creator Mode and Profile Strategy

**Personal profiles outperform company pages**.[4] LinkedIn has limited advanced company analytics to Premium users and expanded verification requirements for companies, recruiters, and executives to improve trust.[4] The algorithm continues to prioritize people over logos in the feed.[4]

## 2025-2026 Algorithm Shifts

The major shift is LinkedIn's transition from rewarding **recency to prioritizing relevance**.[4] LinkedIn intentionally surfaces older posts more frequently to help users not miss important updates like job changes or key professional insights.[4] The platform is investing heavily in AI-driven systems that understand intent, topic, and professional relevance, not just engagement volume.[4]

Collaborative posts and AI-generated content treatment are not addressed in the available search results. A general note: posts matching spam patterns are flagged or deprioritized.[2]

## Tactics to Avoid

**Do not use external links** or the "link in first comment" workaround—both receive severe reach penalties.[3] Avoid generic posts and outreach messages matching spam patterns, as these are flagged or deprioritized by 360Brew.[2] Vague profiles reduce content reach and should be optimized.[1]
