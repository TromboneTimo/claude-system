### (2) Specific Craft Moves for Emotional Captivation

Analyzing the **real excerpts above** (plus similar from Sahil Bloom/Andy Mewborn patterns), here are the 6-8 precise moves that make them feel like gripping stories vs dry explainers. Each tied to excerpt evidence.

- **Opening: Sensory/internal monologue hook (not result tease).** Starts mid-action or raw doubt to pull reader in viscerally—"She was a graphic designer. Burnt out." (Ex1) or "Quiet studio in Ohio. Pieces gathering dust." (Ex2)—evokes texture/smell/weight instantly vs "She succeeded hugely."

- **Internal conflict/doubt: Shown via raw dialogue or fragmented thoughts, not summarized.** Protagonist's voice breaks isolation: ""Justin, I'm done trading time for money. But I have no audience."" (Ex1) or ""I can't sell," he'd say."" (Ex2)—feels confessional, builds empathy without telling "struggled with X."

- **Dialogue vs paraphrase: Heavy protagonist quotes for immediacy/aliveness.** Direct speech like ""Make a video. 60 seconds. Your hands on the clay."" (Ex2) or ""lara, i was invisible. now i'm seen."" (Ex3)—mimics real convo, rhythm like eavesdropping vs "I advised her to..."

- **Pacing: Ultra-short lines + white space + beat breaks for breath/tension.** 1-3 lines max per paragraph, line breaks after dialogue/action:  
  ```
  Week 1: $1,200.
  
  Month 1: $8k.
  ```  
  (Ex1)—mirrors heartbeat, forces scroll pauses vs walls of text.

- **Sensory specifics: Hands/earth/sounds/setting to make alive vs generic.** Clay details—"Sweat. Splinters. The knife slicing wet earth." (Ex2), "Hands in mud. Wheel humming. First light hitting the steam." (Ex3)—taps touch/sound/light; generic would be "made a video."

- **Pivot moment: One tiny, specific action as fulcrum.** Not vague "worked hard"—pinpoint: "Ship one thing. Fast. Ugly is okay." → "10 Canva templates" (Ex1) or "One reel. Your wheel spinning at dawn." (Ex3)—reader sees exact lever.

- **Results: Stacked short bursts, escalating rhythm.** Numbers hit fast: "1M views. Galleries called. 3 exhibitions." (Ex2)—builds dopamine ladder without bullet overwhelm.

- **Lesson: Earned via "Moral:" or protagonist callback, never preached.** Ends implicit: "Just ship." (Ex1) or "Vulnerability + craft = audience." (Ex3)—feels discovered from story vs "You should always..."
