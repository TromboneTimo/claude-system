# TikTok For You Page algorithm best practices 2025-2026: hook conventions in 3 seconds, optimal video length, caption SEO, hashtag strategy, sound selection, posting frequency, suppression triggers

## Key findings

### 1. For You Page Algorithm Signals and TikTok Ranking Factors
TikTok's 2026 FYP algorithm prioritizes **durable attention** through small cohort testing, expanding videos based on watch time, completion rate, replays, shares, comments, follows, and interest signals like saves/favorites, while deshadowing occurs for high-performing niche content via cluster expansion.[1][2][5][6] Key factors include: watch time/completion (strongest signal, e.g., 95% retention beats high views with 30%)[5]; replays/shares (indicate return intent, outweigh likes)[1][2][6]; quality comments (beyond upvotes)[6]; follows from one video (drives profile visits/test phase)[3][5]; interest signals (user clusters from historic behavior match content).[2][4][6] Deshadowing rules favor consistent niche authority, resurfacing older videos on re-trending sounds/topics, but penalize dormancy.[1][5]

### 2. Hook Conventions in the First 3 Seconds, Tested Formulas, Retention Curve Targets
Hooks must capture in 3 seconds via questions, bold claims, visuals, or patterns like "If you [problem], watch this" or "The #1 mistake [niche] makes."[1][5] Tested formulas: loopable openings (rewatchable loops), engineered rewatch moments (surprises at 3-5s), direct search answers via spoken text.[1][2] Target retention curves: 70-95% at 3s, 50-70% to end for expansion; aim for full watches on 15-60s videos to hit viral threshold.[1][3][5][6]

### 3. Optimal Video Length 2025-2026 (Recent Shift to Longer Videos vs Sub-15s)
Shift to **15-60s optimal** (up to 3min for tutorials), favoring longer for durable attention over sub-15s quick hits; algorithm weights full completion of longer videos highest.[1][2][5][7] Sub-15s suits hooks but limits depth; test 21-34s for retention peaks in niches.[1]

### 4. Caption Rules (Search-Optimized Text, Length, Keyword Placement for TikTok Search)
Captions: 100-150 chars max, front-load **keywords/search phrases** (e.g., "How to [query] in 2026"), include questions/CTAs; optimize for auto-captions/transcripts/on-screen text to match TikTok Search/assistant.[1][2][3] Place primary keyword first, niche terms mid; avoid spam.[2]

### 5. Hashtag Strategy 2025-2026 (Post-#FYP Era, Niche Tags, Declining Hashtag Importance)
Post-#FYP era de-emphasizes broad tags like #fyp (declining weight); use **3-5 niche-specific** (e.g., #PersonalFinanceTips2026 over #FYP), focus on metadata/keywords for search over hashtags.[1][2][3][5] Niche tags aid clustering but secondary to captions/audio.[2]

### 6. Sound/Music Selection (Trending Audio vs Original, Copyright)
Prioritize **trending audio** for FYP boost (resurfaces on trends), but original sounds build authority; avoid copyright strikes via TikTok library.[1][2][4][5] Trending > original for discovery; layer voiceover on trends.[1]

### 7. Posting Frequency and Timing
**3-5 videos/week** for consistency (algorithm flags dormancy); peak times: Wed-Fri 2-5 PM local (test analytics), evenings for global.[1][5][6] Rapid experimentation: post-test weekly batches.[1]

### 8. Engagement-Driving CTAs That Work Without Seeming Desperate
Subtle CTAs: "Comment your biggest challenge below," "Save for later," "Share if this hit home," "Follow for weekly tips" – tie to value, post-hook/end.[1][3][6] Avoid "Like if agreeing"; focus on shares/saves for signals.[2]

### 9. Format-Specific Best Practices
- **Talking-head**: Strong eye contact, 15-45s, hook + story + CTA; high completion via pacing.[1][5]
- **B-roll-driven**: Dynamic cuts every 1-2s, trending sound sync, text overlays for search.[1][2]
- **Slideshow photo carousels**: 5-10 images, text hooks, 15-30s; niche education.[5]
- **Lives**: 30-60min, real-time Q&A, schedule recurring; drives follows.[3]
- **Stories/Series**: Episodic (e.g., "Part 1/5"), cliffhangers for replays/series retention.[1][5]

### 10. 2025-2026 Specific Shifts
**TikTok Shop integration**: Tag products in captions/metadata for commerce signals.[2]; **Search engine play**: Reward direct query answers via transcripts.[1][2]; **AI-generated content labeling**: Mandatory labels avoid suppression.[5]; **Longer videos push**: 60s+ weighted for retention over shorts.[1][7]

### 11. Personal Brand Authority on TikTok (Examples: Codie Sanchez, Alex Hormozi, Hala Taha, Jay Shetty)
Build via niche consistency (one topic/video), value-first (tips over sales); e.g., **Codie Sanchez** (contrarian finance hooks, series); **Alex Hormozi** (direct offers, retention via proof); **Hala Taha** (interview clips, search keywords); **Jay Shetty** (wisdom carousels, emotional loops).[1][5][6] All engineer rewatches/shares via authority patterns.

### 12. Suppression Triggers and What NOT to Do
Triggers: low retention (<50%), graphic/safety violations, "not interested" tags, mixed topics, dormancy (>1wk gaps), over-hashtagging, duplicates, AI unlabeled.[1][3][4][5] Avoid: salesy CTAs, off-niche posts, poor hooks, inconsistent posting.[5][6]
