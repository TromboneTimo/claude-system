# Instagram Feed/Reels/Explore algorithm best practices 2025-2026: Mosseri statements, carousel and Reels conventions, caption rules, hashtag de-emphasis, original-content boost, broadcast channels, what NOT to do

## Key findings

### (1) Instagram Algorithm Signals Across Surfaces (Feed, Reels, Explore, Stories)
Instagram uses separate AI-powered systems for **Feed, Reels, Explore, and Stories**, each prioritizing predictions of user satisfaction through distinct signals, as confirmed by Adam Mosseri in 2025 statements[1][3][5]. Across surfaces, the top signals per Mosseri (January 2025) are **watch time (retention), likes per reach, and DM shares (sends per reach)**, with DM shares now the strongest for distribution as they indicate high-value content worth sharing privately[1][2][3][4]. Feed ranks on interaction history, recency, engagement (likes, comments, shares), and negative signals like quick scrolls or skips, predicting spends >10s, profile taps, comments, or reshares[5][6]. Reels emphasize watch time, replays, saves, and DM shares over views or comments[1][2][6]. Explore uses similar audition systems, testing content on small non-follower groups before scaling[2]. Stories prioritize tap likelihood, likes, replies (e.g., emoji reactions, DMs), viewing history, and closeness (e.g., frequent DMs), with action-sparking Stories ranking higher[5][6].

### (2) Reels Best Practices
Create **9:16 aspect ratio** vertical videos with a compelling **cover frame** and **hook in first 1-3 seconds** to combat scroll-past views[1][5]. Target high **retention (watch time > views)** via full watches or replays, as this is the top signal; aim for users stopping scrolls[1][2][3]. **Original content gets a boost** over reposts or watermarked TikTok-style clips, with 2026 AI favoring Instagram-native creativity[1][5][7]. Prioritize **DM shares, saves, and replays** (10x stronger than comments); small accounts succeed if early "audition" groups engage deeply[1][2].

### (3) Carousel Best Practices
Use **7-10 slides** to encourage swipes, as **swipe-back rate** (negative signal if high) impacts ranking—design for forward momentum[1]. Opt for **4:5 or 1:1 aspect** for Feed compatibility, with eye-catching **cover slide** (bold visuals, minimal text)[1]. Limit **text density per slide** to key phrases for readability and retention[1].

### (4) Caption Rules
Place **hook in first line** before "see more" cutoff ( ~125 characters) to boost initial reads[1]. Keep **optimal length 100-150 words** for value without overwhelming; use **line breaks** (2-3 spaces) for scannability, **3-5 emojis** sparingly for emphasis, and **clear CTAs** (e.g., "DM your thoughts," "Save for later") to drive shares/saves[1].

### (5) Hashtag Strategy 2025-2026
**Hashtag discovery is dead**—Instagram explicitly de-emphasized them; use **3-5 niche hashtags** only if hyper-relevant[1][3]. Instead, stuff **alt text with keywords** for SEO and add **location tags** for local reach[1].

### (6) Stories Engagement
Boost ranking with interactive stickers: **polls, sliders, question prompts** signal high reply/tap likelihood, prioritizing them in trays[5][6]. Leverage **emoji reactions and DM replies** as top signals alongside viewing history[5][6].

### (7) Posting Frequency and Content Mix
Post **3-5x/week** total: **50-60% Reels** (for reach), **20-30% carousels** (depth), **10-20% static posts/Stories** daily for connection—balance to hit deep signals without fatigue[1][5]. Collabs 2-4/month amplify[1].

### (8) 2025-2026 Specific Shifts
- **Original-content boost**: AI penalizes reposts/watermarks, rewarding raw, human creativity over polished AI/recycled[1][5][7].
- **Audition system**: Public posts test on small non-followers first; poor retention throttles[2].
- **Broadcast channels/threads**: Emerging for direct engagement (inferred from connection focus)[5].
- **AI labeling**: Platforms verify/label AI vs. real to build trust in authentic creators[7].
- **Swipe-back**: High rates demote carousels[1].
- **Algorithm reset tools** rolled out September 2025 for feed control[3].

### (9) Personal Brand Authority Examples
- **Sahil Bloom, Codie Sanchez**: Master **carousels** (7-10 slides, thread-like storytelling, low-text hooks) for authority in business/wealth niches, driving saves/DMs[1].
- **Justin Welsh**: Solopreneur model via Reels + carousels blending value + CTAs, prioritizing retention/originality for mid-size growth[1].

### (10) Suppression Triggers
- **Engagement bait**: "Like if you agree," "Tag a friend" prompts downranked[5].
- **Watermarks from other platforms** (e.g., TikTok): AI detects and deprioritizes[1][5].
- **Banned/overused hashtags**: Avoid; trigger shadowbans[1].

### (11) What NOT to Do
- Rely on comments/follower count (overcrowded, low weight)[1][2].
- Post generic/polished AI content—lean raw/authentic[7].
- Ignore early retention (first 3s hooks essential)[1][2].
- Overuse hashtags (>5) or grid aesthetics (irrelevant now)[1][3].
- Repost without edits; chase views over watch time[1][5].
