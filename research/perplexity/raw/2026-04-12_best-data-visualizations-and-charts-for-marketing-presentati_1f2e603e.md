# Best data visualizations and charts for marketing presentations. When to use bar charts, line graphs, pie charts, scatter plots, comparison tables, infographics, before/after visua

## Key findings

**Bar charts** excel at comparing discrete categories or groups, such as conversion rates across marketing channels; use them when showing differences in quantities between 2-10 items to avoid clutter.[8][5]

**Line graphs** track changes and trends over time, ideal for metrics like website traffic growth or campaign performance month-over-month.[8][1]

**Pie charts** display parts of a whole for simple proportions (limit to 5-7 slices), but avoid for precise comparisons due to perceptual distortion; better for quick shares like market segment breakdowns.[8][4]

**Scatter plots** reveal relationships or correlations between two variables, such as ad spend vs. ROI, highlighting outliers or clusters.[5]

**Comparison tables** organize multi-dimensional data side-by-side for easy scanning, like tool features or campaign KPIs across platforms.[2]

**Infographics** combine charts, icons, and text for storytelling, perfect for complex narratives like full marketing funnels; tools like Infogram support real-time data and customization.[3][6]

**Before/after visuals** (e.g., side-by-side bars or slides) demonstrate impact, such as engagement pre- and post-AI tool adoption.[6]

**Stat callouts** isolate single impactful metrics with large fonts, icons, or animations for emphasis, like "300% ROI lift".[6]
