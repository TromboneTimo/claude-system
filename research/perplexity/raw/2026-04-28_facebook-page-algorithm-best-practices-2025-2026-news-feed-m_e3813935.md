# Facebook Page algorithm best practices 2025-2026: News Feed MSI signals, Page reach decline, Reels push, post-format performance, ad-boost economics, AI content treatment, Meta Verified

## Key findings

### (1) Facebook News Feed Algorithm Signals 2025-2026
The 2026 Facebook News Feed algorithm prioritizes **meaningful social interactions (MSI)**—long, threaded comments (10+ words) from friends or known connections over likes or reactions from strangers—as the dominant signal, with MSI scoring determining relevance via AI-driven predictions of engagement likelihood.[1][2] **MSI scoring** weighs comment depth and relationships highest, alongside **time-on-content** (dwell time on posts/comments) and **video completion** (crossing 60-second thresholds boosts promotion disproportionately).[1][2] The **Reels push** continues aggressively, with Reels outperforming feed posts 5–10x due to watch time, rewatches, and shares as top signals, shifting from follow-graph to interest-graph distribution.[1][2]

### (2) Post-Format Performance: Reach Data 2025-2026
Reels dominate with **5–10x higher reach** than other formats on Pages, driven by discovery and watch time; native video follows via completion rates (full watches lift distribution 10x).[1][2] Image and text status posts lag due to low MSI; link shares see suppressed reach; live video underperforms without strong first-hour engagement (<2% Page organic benchmark).[2] No 2026-specific reach percentages available, but Pages average <2% organic reach overall, with Reels as the exception.[2]

### (3) Page vs. Personal Profile Reach Gap; Organic Reach Benchmarks 2025-2026
**Page organic reach** continues its long decline to <2% in 2026, as the algorithm favors friends, Groups, and creator-led personal profiles (10x+ lift for founder/creator accounts).[1][2] Personal profiles retain higher reach via stronger relationship signals; Pages rely on Reels or Groups for parity, with feed posts rarely escaping the "disappear" threshold.[1][2]

### (4) Hook Conventions for FB Feed (Mobile-First)
Craft **mobile-first hooks** in the **first 100-125 characters** (before "see more") to halt scrolling: use questions, bold claims, or emotional triggers predicting stop-and-read behavior.[1] For videos/Reels, the **first 1.5 seconds** must hook via visuals/audio, as watch time is the strongest signal.[1][2]

### (5) Optimal Post Length, Video Duration, Hashtag Use
**Post length**: Keep text concise for full visibility (under 125 chars ideal); longer for MSI via comment prompts.[1] **Video duration**: 60+ seconds for feed completion boosts; Reels optimize 10–60 seconds with loop-friendly edits for rewatches.[1][2] **Hashtags**: Mostly cosmetic on Facebook—use 1–3 for discoverability, but prioritize content signals over volume.[1]

### (6) Posting Frequency and Timing (Hootsuite, Sprout Social Benchmarks 2025-2026)
No direct 2026 Hootsuite/Sprout Social benchmarks in results, but algorithm spacing prevents monotony: **2–3 posts/day max** for Pages to avoid suppression, focusing on peak user times (e.g., evenings for MSI).[1] Prioritize quality over frequency; Reels can post daily without penalty due to separate distribution.[1][2]

### (7) Engagement-Driving Tactics Surviving Engagement-Bait Penalty
Encourage **genuine MSI** via threaded reply prompts (e.g., "What's your take?"), not "like if..." bait, which triggers penalties.[1][2] Leverage relationships (tag past engagers), trends in Reels, and dwell time via rich comments—first-hour velocity amplifies without spamming.[1][2]

### (8) Reels-on-Facebook Strategy 2025-2026
**Reels reach far exceeds feed posts** (5–10x on Pages), fueled by watch time > completion rate, rewatches, DM shares, and saves—optimize sequence: 1.5s hook → retention → loop → share trigger.[1][2] Use original content (no watermarks), trends contextually, and consistent themes for interest-graph targeting; post daily for compounding discovery.[1][2]

### (9) Facebook Groups vs. Pages for Personal Brand Authority
**Groups outperform Pages** for authority and reach in 2026, receiving distribution boosts as the "Groups-first" experience; build owned Groups for MSI and organic growth over Page reliance.[2] Pages suit broadcasting; Groups excel for personal brands via discussions.[1][2]

### (10) Ad-Boost Economics (Boost vs. Organic Growth)
Boost Reels or high-MSI posts when organic stalls (<2% reach), but prioritize organic via Groups/Reels first—boost economics favor 5–10x Reel amplification at low cost if watch time >60%.[1][2] Avoid boosting low-engagement feed posts; grow organically with creator profiles.[2]

### (11) 2025-2026 Specific Shifts
**AI content treatment**: Algorithms detect low-quality/recycled AI via signals, limiting reach—favor original, high-edit videos.[1] **Meta Verified blue check**: Likely boosts creator distribution (inferred from creator-led preference), though unconfirmed.[2] **Reels push**: Aggressive 5–10x prioritization vs. feed.[1][2] **Threads cross-posting**: Supports interest-graph via shares (untested in results).[2] **Live Audio Rooms decline**: No mention; focus shifted to video/Live video with completion thresholds.[1][2]

### (12) Suppression Triggers and What NOT to Do
Avoid: engagement bait ("comment yes!"), spammy repetition, watermarked/recycled content, guideline violations, over-posting (monotonous feeds), or link-heavy shares—all trigger relevance score drops or removal.[1][2] Steer clear of generic trends without context; prioritize first-hour MSI over volume.[1][2]
