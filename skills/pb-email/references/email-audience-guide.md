# Email audience guide

Four audience tags. Each shapes the agent assignments and the body length expectations.

## broadcast

**Targets:** the whole AC list (active subscribers, no exclusions).

**Use for:** newsletter, announcement, content drop, story-led re-engagement that feels like a regular send.

**Body length:** 350 to 600 words.

**Hook angle bias:** identity, specific-result, curiosity. Avoid hard money pitch as primary angle; broadcasts that feel like ads underperform.

**CTA bias:** training-rewatch, youtube-watch, occasionally strategy-session.

**P.S. type bias:** social-pressure (low) or risk-reversal (low) work best. Skip scarcity unless real.

**Agents most relevant:** Sales-call anchor (Agent 1), Testimonial proof (Agent 2), Fresh lens (Agent 6).

## reengagement

**Targets:** subscribers cold for 30, 60, or 90 days (no opens or clicks).

**Use for:** win-back. Pattern-interrupt. "Did this miss you?"

**Body length:** 250 to 450 words. Shorter is better for cold subscribers.

**Hook angle bias:** failed-method, curiosity. Identity works if testimonial-anchored.

**CTA bias:** discovery-call, training-rewatch. Soft. Do not push the strategy session as the first reactivation step.

**P.S. type bias:** risk-reversal works best. Avoid urgency on cold subscribers; it reads as desperate.

**Agents most relevant:** Sales-call anchor (Agent 1), Objection preempt (Agent 3), Fresh lens (Agent 6).

## webinar-push

**Targets:** cold list driving to free training opt-in. Mirrors emails 1 and 2 of the existing webinar sequence.

**Use for:** filling the training. Numbers game.

**Body length:** 400 to 700 words. Includes the "we'll cover" promise list (Section 7b of voice catalog) and the qualifier (Section 7e).

**Hook angle bias:** specific-result, money. Identity hooks too vague for webinar push.

**CTA bias:** strategy-session (after watching training). Use the Step 1 / Step 2 stack from Section 7a of the voice catalog.

**P.S. type bias:** scarcity, timing, urgency. Webinar push tolerates harder P.S.

**Agents most relevant:** P.S. trigger (Agent 5), Winning-pattern (Agent 4), Sales-call anchor (Agent 1).

## discovery-followup

**Targets:** prospects who booked a discovery or strategy call but did not show, OR showed but did not close.

**Use for:** re-warm. Reduce shame. Restate value.

**Body length:** 200 to 400 words. Personal feel.

**Hook angle bias:** identity, curiosity. Avoid failed-method if Harrison was the call.

**CTA bias:** discovery-call (rebook). Always rebook, not "buy now".

**P.S. type bias:** risk-reversal exclusively. No scarcity.

**Agents most relevant:** Discovery-followup (Agent 6 in followup mode), Testimonial proof (Agent 2), Objection preempt (Agent 3).

## When Timo picks multiple audiences

The skill spawns ONE menu of 5 drafts per `/pb-email` run. If Timo picks multiple audiences (e.g. "broadcast and reengagement"), the auditor (Agent 7) ensures the final 5 covers both:
- At least 1 draft per audience
- Remaining 3 slots filled by whichever audience needs more reps

If Timo wants 5 broadcast drafts AND 5 re-engagement drafts, run `/pb-email` twice with separate audience inputs. Do not try to fit 10 drafts in one menu.
