# Tim Maines Voice Methodology Research Synthesis

Consolidated methodology for discovering and codifying the writing voice of a brand-new "Tim Maines" personal brand channel that starts from zero, has no published sample posts, and refuses to fabricate samples upfront. Voice will emerge through structured interviews plus a measured publish-and-refine loop. All claims cite source URLs.

## 1. Cold-Start Methodology (No Sample Posts Yet)

The cold-start problem: every voice-replication framework in the ghostwriting literature assumes existing written samples to mine for stylometric patterns. When the human refuses to write fake samples upfront, the substitute is a structured discovery interview combined with an iterative publish loop. This pairs the ghostwriter intake process (source 1) with the WRITE-PUBLISH-MEASURE-REFINE cycle (source 4) so the spec is seeded before post 1 and refined live as posts ship.

### Phase A: Discovery Interview as Sample Substitute

Top ghostwriting firms capture authentic voice from non-writers through recorded interviews that get transcribed and analyzed for speech patterns, vocabulary, anecdote style, and sentence structure (https://ghostwritingllc.com/blog/top-10-ghostwriting-services-for-personal-branding/, https://www.adeptghostwriting.com/blog/top-10-self-help-ghostwriting-companies/). Scribe Media's interview-based extraction is built specifically for executives and other non-writers who cannot produce samples on demand: a trained interviewer draws out expertise verbally, the responses are transcribed, and the manuscript is shaped to mimic the speaker's natural voice (https://www.adeptghostwriting.com/blog/top-10-self-help-ghostwriting-companies/, https://www.verityghostwriting.com/blog/top-10-ghostwriting-services-in-usa/). Oscar Ghostwriting runs phase 1 as in-depth recorded consultations capturing vision, goals, and unique tone, followed by transcribed outlines approved by the client (https://www.oscarghostwriting.com/blog/top-10-ghostwriting-companies-in-the-world/). Phoenix Ghostwriting records brief executive interviews specifically to mimic speech elements when ghostwriting C-suite articles (https://www.phoenixghostwriting.com/blog/best-firms-for-ghostwriting-and-positioning-c-suite-articles/).

The interview substitutes for sample posts. Instead of asking "show me your last 10 posts," you interrogate the human until you have a 60 to 90 minute transcript dense enough to fingerprint.

**Discovery questions to ask Tim (designed to surface speech patterns, not opinions):**

1. Tell me the story of how you got here. Walk me through it the way you would tell a friend at a bar.
2. What is the one belief you hold about your industry that almost everyone else gets wrong? Now defend it as if I disagreed.
3. What is the dumbest piece of advice you keep hearing in your space? Roast it.
4. Describe your last big win. Then describe your last big failure. Same level of detail for both.
5. If you had to teach a complete beginner the one thing that actually matters, what would you say first? What would you say second?
6. Who is the enemy of the people you want to help? Name them. (Welsh's framing about "solopreneur enemies" surfaces polarization, which is what voice-discovery engagement signals key on, per https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZkwYxYfkfYU.)
7. Tell me about a client moment that pissed you off. Tell me one that made you proud.
8. What do you sound like when you are angry on this topic? Demonstrate.
9. What do you say in a voice memo to yourself that you would never say in a marketing email?

These are designed to elicit varied registers (story, polemic, teaching, anger, tenderness) rather than a single tone. Recording across registers is what lets the spec capture range, not just a single midpoint.

### Phase B: Voice Fingerprint Dimensions to Extract from the Transcript

Ghostwriting LLC analyzes transcripts for "vocabulary tier, pet phrases, and rhythm" (https://ghostwritingllc.com/blog/top-10-ghostwriting-services-for-personal-branding/). Phoenix and Vox extend this with patterns like rhythm and recurring phrases pulled from the audio (https://www.phoenixghostwriting.com/blog/best-firms-for-ghostwriting-and-positioning-c-suite-articles/). Stylometric analysis of speech transcripts (sentence-length distribution, contraction frequency, punctuation tics) is the inferred industry-standard quantification layer (https://ghostwritingllc.com/blog/top-10-ghostwriting-services-for-personal-branding/).

**Six dimensions to capture from the Tim transcript before post 1 ships:**

1. **Rhythm**: average sentence length, standard deviation, longest-to-shortest ratio, paragraph cadence (does Tim deliver in 3-beat bursts, in long arcs that resolve, or in choppy single-line punches).
2. **Vocabulary tier**: Hemingway-app readability target (Welsh recommends grade 1, per https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SkR3XB6Bvq8), domain jargon allow-list, abstraction ceiling.
3. **Pet phrases**: 5 to 15 recurring openers, transitions, and closers Tim uses verbally that should survive into posts verbatim.
4. **Contraction frequency**: ratio of contracted to expanded forms ("don't" vs "do not"). Casual voice usually runs above 70 percent contracted; formal voice runs below 30 percent.
5. **Sentence-length distribution**: histogram of word counts per sentence. Most personal-brand voices that read as authentic show a bimodal distribution (lots of short hits, occasional long arcs, very few mediums).
6. **Punctuation tics**: em-dash usage (banned for Tim), ellipses, parenthetical asides, all-caps emphasis frequency, single-sentence paragraphs.

The output is a v0.1 voice spec written before any post ships. It is explicitly a hypothesis, not a contract.

### Phase C: WRITE-PUBLISH-MEASURE-REFINE Iteration

Justin Welsh's publicly documented loop (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SkR3XB6Bvq8, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7laZlckl2i4):

- **WRITE**: ideate from personal stories and problems-plus-solutions. Use the structure of hook with pain or agitation, add intrigue via story, deliver solution, end with nugget plus call to action. Run drafts through Hemingway App for grade-1 readability (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SkR3XB6Bvq8).
- **PUBLISH**: ship daily or weekly short-form. Hub-and-spoke model turns one long-form idea into ten pieces (one newsletter or podcast becomes thirty social posts) (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SkR3XB6Bvq8, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7laZlckl2i4).
- **MEASURE**: track what works (engagement, saves, comments) versus what does not. Double down on compelling and polarizing formats (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7laZlckl2i4).
- **REFINE**: cut failures. Iterate on copywriting (Welsh cites Ogilvy and Russell Brunson as study material). Practice communicating value until specific-versus-viral balance emerges (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7laZlckl2i4, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZkwYxYfkfYU).

Top ghostwriting firms encode the same cycle as "write-react-codify": draft from interview, client reacts, codify the reaction (e.g. "anecdotes always end with a one-line gut-punch") into the spec, revise (https://ghostwritingllc.com/blog/top-10-ghostwriting-services-for-personal-branding/, https://www.phoenixghostwriting.com/blog/best-firms-for-ghostwriting-and-positioning-c-suite-articles/, https://www.imperialghostwriting.com/blog/top-10-best-ghostwriting-services-in-united-states/).

**Voice stabilizes between posts 30 and 100** (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7laZlckl2i4). Bush's Ship 30 for 30 program targets the 30-day mark explicitly via daily Atomic Essays. Welsh, Bloom, Walls, Denning, and Cole all document voice emerging around the 50 to 100 post range via volume (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7laZlckl2i4).

**How the loop refines the spec post by post:**

- After every 5 posts, diff what Tim actually wrote against the v0.1 spec. Three patterns emerge: spec held, spec was wrong, spec was missing a rule.
- After every 15 posts, re-rank the engagement winners. The top 3 by save rate and DM quality (not likes) become the new prototype patterns. Update the spec to encode their structural commonalities.
- At post 30, draft v0.2 of the spec. By post 100, v1.0 should be stable enough that a ghostwriter could draft a passable post without Tim seeing it first.

## 2. NN/g 4-Dimension Framework as Default Scaffolding

Nielsen Norman Group's framework is the only tone-of-voice methodology with concrete details across the source set; Mailchimp, Atlassian, Buffer, StoryBrand, GatherContent, and CMI returned no usable details (https://www.nngroup.com/videos/tone-of-voice-dimensions/, https://www.copystyleguide.com/how-it-works). Use NN/g as the default scaffolding for the v0.1 spec.

**Four bipolar axes** (https://www.nngroup.com/videos/tone-of-voice-dimensions/, https://maestrolearning.com/blogs/tone-of-voice-examples/, https://doctoolhub.com/resource/the-four-dimensions-of-tone-of-voice-norman-nielson-group/):

1. **Funny vs Serious** (F vs S)
2. **Formal vs Casual** (F vs C)
3. **Respectful vs Irreverent** (R vs I)
4. **Enthusiastic vs Matter-of-fact** (E vs M)

Each axis is plotted qualitatively from existing brand content (or, in cold start, from the discovery transcript) (https://www.nngroup.com/videos/tone-of-voice-dimensions/, https://snowball.digital/blog/tone-of-voice-the-new-level-of-branding).

**16 profile combinations**, e.g. "Earnest Ally" = Casual + Serious + Respectful + Enthusiastic (CSRE), or "Insightful Disruptor" = Formal + Funny + Irreverent + Matter-of-fact (FFIM) (https://www.copystyleguide.com/how-it-works, https://maestrolearning.com/blogs/tone-of-voice-examples/).

**Iteration is not specified in the core framework**; Oli West's 2023 adaptation suggests re-evaluating content against profiles after testing (https://www.copystyleguide.com/how-it-works). For Tim, this maps onto the post-30 spec rev: replot the 4 axes from actual published posts, compare to the v0.1 plot, and document the drift.

## 3. Per-Platform Writing Rules (Named Creators)

Every top creator maintains a core voice while shifting length, structure, and density per platform (https://www.creatoriq.com/blog/6-creator-marketing-shifts-to-watch-for-in-2026, https://www.fitlifecreation.com/post/10-creator-trends-for-personal-brands-and-creator-ceos-in-2026-creator-empire-era).

| Platform | Named-creator rules |
|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | Welsh: 5-part LinkedIn OS (Hook, Context, Dialogue, Story, Advice), 500 to 1k words, "solopreneur mentor" first-person pragmatism, consultative B2B (https://learn.justinwelsh.me/linkedin-operating-system). Bush: expand Atomic Essays with career context (https://ship30for30.com/). Sanchez: add business case studies on top of contrarian hook (https://contrarianthinking.co/). |
| **X / Twitter** | Welsh: condense LinkedIn OS to thread hook plus advice (https://learn.justinwelsh.me/linkedin-operating-system). Bush: deploy full Atomic Essays as standalone tweets, claim plus proof plus punchline in 200 to 500 words (https://ship30for30.com/). Abdaal: numbered-insight threads, personal experiments, end with engagement question, "productive med student" voice (https://aliabdaal.com/resources/). Sanchez: teaser hooks plus polls for debates (https://contrarianthinking.co/). Bloom: break essays into tweet-sized mental models (https://www.sahilbloom.com/newsletter). Hormozi: Value Equation applied to niches per thread (https://www.acquisition.com/training). |
| **TikTok** | Bush: excerpt punchlines for virality (https://ship30for30.com/). Sanchez: "acquisitions for normies" soundbites (https://contrarianthinking.co/). Hormozi: simplify to "equation plus CTA" (https://www.acquisition.com/training). Vee: hype captions, high-energy repetition pulled from pillar content (https://garyvaynerchuk.com/content-model/). |
| **Instagram (captions)** | Welsh: story snippets plus CTAs (https://learn.justinwelsh.me/linkedin-operating-system). Abdaal: 1 to 2 productivity hacks plus emojis (https://aliabdaal.com/resources/). Bloom: highlight one actionable takeaway (https://www.sahilbloom.com/newsletter). Vee: motivational hype captions repurposed from pillar (https://garyvaynerchuk.com/content-model/). |
| **YouTube descriptions** | Welsh: full templates (https://learn.justinwelsh.me/linkedin-operating-system). Bush: link essay series (https://ship30for30.com/). Abdaal: timestamp threads tied to video sections (https://aliabdaal.com/resources/). Sanchez: include resource lists (https://contrarianthinking.co/). Hormozi: detail the full Value Equation breakdown (https://www.acquisition.com/training). Vee: descriptive recap of pillar content (https://garyvaynerchuk.com/content-model/). |
| **Facebook** | Vee: pillar-to-cookie repurposing, motivational documents and hype scaling, high-energy repetition (https://garyvaynerchuk.com/content-model/). Welsh and others do not document Facebook-specific shifts in available sources. |

The cross-platform pattern: voice is preserved through tone fingerprint (Welsh's "solopreneur mentor," Bush's "writer's-block destroyer," Sanchez's "deal-making rebel," Abdaal's "evidence-based optimism," Bloom's "curious investor," Hormozi's "no-BS scaler with math precision," Vee's "hustle empath"); structure flexes by platform.

## 4. Success Signals (You Have Found Voice When)

Per Welsh (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZkwYxYfkfYU, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7laZlckl2i4):

- **Engagement**: likes and comments spike specifically on polarizing or specific-niche topics (e.g. naming an enemy of the audience). Generic engagement is noise; polarizing engagement is signal (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZkwYxYfkfYU).
- **Save rate**: short nuggets that get re-read are a stronger signal than likes (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7laZlckl2i4).
- **DM quality**: inbound DMs shift from generic compliments to revenue-aligned inquiries (questions about products, services, or working together) (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZkwYxYfkfYU).

If post 50 is generating saves and revenue-aligned DMs, voice is found. If post 100 is still generating only likes, the spec is wrong and needs a v0.2.
