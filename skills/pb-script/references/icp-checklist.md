# ICP Checklist (Harrison Ball / Precision Brass)

Run every candidate idea through these 5 questions. If ANY answer is "no" or "unclear", reject the idea or rewrite until it passes.

## The 5 Gate Questions

### 1. Does this speak directly to someone aged 40-65?

The trumpet content market is mostly aimed at college students, jazz aspirants, and middle-school band kids. Harrison's customer is the OPPOSITE of that. Older. Returning. Often with money. Often with a wound.

If the idea would also fit in a Berklee Trumpet 101 syllabus, it's wrong.

If you're unsure, ask: would a 22-year-old jazz student click on this title? If yes, rewrite.

### 2. Does it address a specific Precision Brass pain point?

The five canonical pains:
- **Range loss** (lost upper register, can't hit notes they used to)
- **Endurance collapse** (tires after 20 minutes, can't last a full gig)
- **Mouthpiece confusion** (chasing equipment instead of fixing technique)
- **Age anxiety** (dental work, lip damage, thinning embouchure)
- **Comeback player frustration** (came back after 10-30 year break, can't get back to where they were)

The idea must name ONE of these specifically. "Improve your trumpet playing" is not specific.

### 3. Is it targeted to US-based players?

Harrison's customer is overwhelmingly American. Cultural references, dollar pricing, jazz/big-band/orchestral idioms that resonate in the US specifically.

The idea should feel like it's spoken from inside that culture. Not generic-international.

### 4. Would someone OUTSIDE Harrison's ICP want to watch this?

This is the inverse test. If a 19-year-old college trumpet major would also click on the title, the idea is too generic.

If yes to "outside ICP would want this", rewrite to make it MORE Harrison-specific. Add the comeback frame. Add the age frame. Add the business-owner frame. Add the failed-method-history frame.

### 5. Does the idea promise an identity outcome, not a technique outcome?

Wrong: "How to play high G consistently"
Right: "Reclaim the range you had at 25, without re-learning trumpet"

Wrong: "Embouchure tutorial for adults"
Right: "Why your old teacher's embouchure advice broke your face (and what working embouchures actually feel like)"

Wrong: "Endurance exercises"
Right: "The reason your chops die at minute 20 (and why doubling your practice time made it worse)"

The promise must be about **who they become** or **what they recover**, not what skill they learn.

## Domino check (additional)

Each idea must shatter exactly ONE limiting belief. Not three. Not zero.

Examples of single-belief targets:
- "I'm too old to get my range back"
- "Mouthpiece swapping is the answer to my range problem"
- "I just need to practice more"
- "My dental work ruined my embouchure for good"
- "I've tried every method, none of them work for me"

If the idea covers 3 beliefs, narrow it. One video, one belief.

## Pass = ALL 5 yeses + 1 belief targeted. Anything less = reject or rewrite.
