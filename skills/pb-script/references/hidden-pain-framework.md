# Hidden Pain Framework

Used by Agent 2 (Hidden Pain Hunter). Surfaces problems prospects feel but haven't named. Naming the unnamed pain is the highest-converting hook pattern in the corpus (see Agent A's verified-winner deconstruction).

## The 8 hidden problems

Sourced from `/Users/air/Desktop/Precision-Brass/context/prospect-psychology.md` (19 prospect dives). Each one is a real wound prospects circle around in sales calls without ever naming directly.

| ID | Hidden problem | What they SAY (surface) | What they FEEL (real wound) |
|---|---|---|---|
| HP1 | Identity threat of being fixed | "I just want to play better" | "If I can be fixed in 6 weeks, then 30 years of struggle was wasted, and what does that say about me?" |
| HP2 | Aging anxiety disguised as technique | "My range is going" | "I'm watching my body decline and trumpet is my proof of life" |

| HP8 | Too-old self-prophecy | "I'm 58, is this realistic?" | "I'm pre-emptively granting myself permission to fail by making age the cause" |

## How to surface subtext

The agent reads sales call transcripts and the deep psychological dive looking for **circling**: prospect mentions a topic 3+ times in different framings without naming the underlying wound. That circling pattern is the signature of hidden pain.

### Detection patterns

1. **Compensation clauses**: "I know I should be able to, but...", "It's not that I can't, it's just...", "I used to be able to..."
2. **Hypothetical distancing**: "Some guys at my age...", "A friend of mine said...", "You hear about people who..."
3. **Excessive technical detail in non-technical questions**: prospect drowns the emotional question in mouthpiece specs.
4. **Apologizing for the goal**: "I know it sounds crazy, but I want to play first chair again"
5. **Repeated reassurance-seeking**: "Is that even possible at my age?" asked 3+ times.

### Example (real, anonymized)

Prospect says: "I've tried mouthpieces. I've tried more air. My teacher said tighten the corners. Nothing's worked. I'm probably just at my ceiling."

Surface read: technique frustration. (Boring, every coach addresses this.)

Subtext read (HP3 + HP1): the prospect has stopped believing they can improve, AND they're afraid that if Harrison fixes them in 6 weeks, the prior 20 years of effort were wasted. The video that converts here doesn't teach a new exercise. It says: "Your 20 years weren't wasted. They were just aimed at the wrong target. Here's what was off."

That reframe handles BOTH HP1 (identity preservation) and HP3 (rekindle hope) in one move. That's a hidden-pain idea.
