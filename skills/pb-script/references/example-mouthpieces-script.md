# Example Script: "Stop Buying Mouthpieces. Your Top Lip Is the Problem."

This is the proven structural template that emerged from the 2026-04-25 iteration with Timo. Use it as a model for future Harrison long-form scripts. The structure interleaves trap topic + named mistake + diagnostic test in single beats so each beat is a complete narrative cycle.

## The 7-beat interleaved structure

| Beat | Color  | Title | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | Coral  | Cold Open + Hook | Mission Impossible visual gag, locked hook line, comeback/age callout, demo promise |
| 02 | Blue   | The Demo | Part 1 (3-6 mouthpieces same tune) + Part 2 (1-3 mouthpieces with shitty setup) |
| 03 | Green  | Apparatus Reveal | Mouthpiece reframe, BEAT, "wasn't the mouthpiece, was the apparatus" reveal, definition |
| 04 | Mustard | Mistake 1: The Mouthpiece Reset | The 99% Lie -> The Mistake -> The Test (woven) |
| 05 | Plum   | Mistake 2: The Pressure Mash | Dead Methods -> The Mistake -> The Test (woven) |
| 06 | Magenta | Mistake 3: The Reactive Embouchure | Why I Built VAS -> The Mistake -> The Test (woven) |
| 07 | Teal   | Funnel | Callback to tests + NEW provocative question + Hint pattern + embouchure video CTA |

## Locked lines (must remain verbatim)

**Cold open:**
> "If you've spent more than $500 on mouthpieces in the last five years and you still can't hit a clean high C at the end of a tune, you don't have a mouthpiece problem. You have a setup problem. And in this video you're going to learn the three common mistakes my students make with their setup, and why a new mouthpiece won't fix any of them. Which I'll first demonstrate by playing the same tune on three different mouthpieces. See if you can hear the difference."

**Apparatus reveal (after mouthpiece reframe + BEAT):**
> "What you just heard wasn't the mouthpiece. It was the playing apparatus."

**Mistake 1 callout:**
> "That's how you fall into the first mistake, which is what I call the Mouthpiece Reset."

**Mistake 2 callout:**
> "That's where the second mistake comes from. The Pressure Mash."

**Mistake 3 callout:**
> "Because the deepest problem most of you have is the third mistake. The Reactive Embouchure."

**Funnel close (uses converter's Hint pattern):**
> "Now, if you struggled with any of those tests, here's the bigger question none of them actually answer. WHERE on your face is the mouthpiece supposed to sit? ... Hint: it's not where 99% of teachers tell you. I broke it all down in this video right here. Make sure you watch that next."

## Per-cycle structure (Beats 4-6)

Each cycle has THREE labeled sections inside one beat:
1. **The Trap** (named: "The 99% Lie" / "Dead Methods" / "Why I Built VAS")
   Action: Harrison to camera. Riff the trap topic in 2-4 paragraphs.
2. **The Mistake** (named: "The Mouthpiece Reset" / "The Pressure Mash" / "The Reactive Embouchure")
   One paragraph naming the mistake explicitly. Use "the first/second/third mistake".
3. **The Test**
   Action: visual demonstration. One paragraph riff explaining what the test reveals.

## Why this structure works

It mirrors the converter's 12-move template AND adds three diagnostic tests woven into the trap explanation. The viewer experiences:
1. Visual contradiction (Beat 2 demo proves mouthpieces don't matter as much as expected)
2. The pivot (Beat 3 names the apparatus as the real cause)
3. Three named diagnoses with self-tests (Beats 4-6) so the viewer immediately knows which mistake is theirs
4. Funnel that opens a NEW knowledge gap (WHERE the mouthpiece goes) which the embouchure video answers

## How to model future scripts on this

When generating Phase 2 scripts for new ideas:
- Always interleave trap topics + mistakes + tests within beats. Never stack them as separate sections.
- Use the converter's "Hint:" pattern in the funnel CTA.
- Funnel must point to the proven embouchure video AND open a NEW question/gap that motivates the click.
- Locked lines for the hook, the apparatus reveal, the mistake callouts, and the funnel CTA. Riff topics for everything else.
- Each beat color must be distinct so Harrison can find his place at a glance.
- Visual gag in the cold open (like Mission Impossible) creates curiosity before any words are said.
