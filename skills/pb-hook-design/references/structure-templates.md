# Body structure templates (the 6 beats after the hook)

Top 3 hook-scoring Antoine videos used three distinct body shapes. Pick the body shape based on the hook archetype chosen in Step 3 of pb-hook-design.

## Template A. "X Levels" diagnostic ladder (from PcsnUkruHks, 46/60, 67,970 views)

The viewer walks up a ladder of mastery levels and self-diagnoses where they are.

| Beat | Time | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:30 | Hook (Pattern 1, 2, or 4) plus name the N levels |
| 2 | 0:30-1:30 | Why people stay stuck. Why level X feels like a wall. Authority story or short demo |
| 3 | 1:30-3:00 | Level 1 with on-instrument demo. What it sounds like. The exercise to graduate it |
| 4 | 3:00-4:30 | Level 2 with demo and graduation exercise |
| 5 | 4:30-6:30 | Levels 3-N with progressively shorter demos. Optional Level N showcase (the legend playing) |
| 6 | 6:30-end | CTA. "Comment what level you're on" + free masterclass link |

**Use for Harrisson topics like:**
- "The 4 (or 5) levels of trumpet high range"
- "The 3 stages of getting your chops back"
- "5 levels of trumpet endurance"

**Body-content rule:** each level must include a 1-line "you know you're here when..." plus an exercise. No vague levels.

## Template B. "Named Framework + N Beats" (from KBT0roUguhc 44/60, sLpOh6wpQZs 43/60, Ox0V5_ZnF4c 40/60)

The viewer learns a named multi-part framework. Each beat = one named element with a demo.

| Beat | Time | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:30 | Hook (Pattern 1 or 3 typically) plus name the framework + preview N elements |
| 2 | 0:30-1:30 | Why most teachers get this wrong. The gatekeeping reveal lives here, NOT in the hook |
| 3 | 1:30-3:00 | Element 1 named. Demonstrate. What viewer should see. Common mistake |
| 4 | 3:00-4:30 | Element 2 named. Demonstrate. Mistake to avoid |
| 5 | 4:30-6:30 | Element 3 (and 4 if applicable) demonstrated. Apply on a real piece of music |
| 6 | 6:30-end | CTA to free masterclass + question for the comments |

**Use for Harrisson topics like:**
- "The 4 Points of Contact" (Harrisson's existing named technique)
- "The 3 Factors Behind a Beautiful High Range"
- "Vertical Alignment + Mouthpiece Placement: the 2 secrets"

**Body-content rule:** each element gets a NAMED label (not "the first thing" but "vertical alignment" or "gravity breath"). Names are the brandable hook of the body.

## Template C. Story-led documentary (from J0OdaOyrS1c, 46/60)

The hook IS a story (Pattern 2). The body unpacks the story into actionable levels or steps.

| Beat | Time | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00-0:30 | Story hook (Paganini, a real student, a specific legend). End with "but the real reason was..." |
| 2 | 0:30-1:30 | Reveal: the story's hidden mechanism. Connect to viewer's life |
| 3 | 1:30-3:00 | Step 1 of the method. Walk through what [legend / student] actually did |
| 4 | 3:00-5:00 | Step 2. Show on instrument |
| 5 | 5:00-7:00 | Step 3 (and the harder advanced step if it fits). Optional showcase of the result |
| 6 | 7:00-end | CTA. "Want the full system? Free masterclass" |

**Use for Harrisson topics like:**
- "How Maynard actually built his range"
- "This 58-year-old comeback player went from G to double C in 9 months. Here's how"
- "The hidden practice habit every lead trumpet player uses"

**Body-content rule:** the story is a vehicle. Each step must be replicable by the viewer, not just an interesting fact about the legend.

## Beat-1 timing rule (applies to all templates)

The hook is 0:00-0:30. Not 0:00-1:00. Antoine's winners all cut to body content before 35 seconds. If the hook draft runs past 35 seconds at typical reading speed (~180 wpm = ~105 words for 35s), cut.

## CTA rule (applies to all templates)

Antoine's CTAs always include:
1. A specific verbal redirect: "I show this in detail in the free masterclass."
2. A comment prompt: "What level are you on right now?" or "Which point feels broken for you?"
3. A "watch this video next" prompt to a related video.

For Harrisson:
- Masterclass redirect: precisionbrass.info masterclass URL (canonical, per [[project-canonical-links]])
- Comment prompt: pick the question that best fits the body shape
- Next-video prompt: link to the embouchure converter or whichever existing PB video the funnel naturally feeds

## Visual plan rule (applies to all templates)

Per `[[project-pb-script-write]]` voice protocol, every beat must specify what the viewer SEES. Antoine demonstrates EVERY framework element on the violin. Harrisson must demonstrate every point/level/factor on the trumpet, with at least:
- One close-up of embouchure or hand position
- One full-body shot for context
- One sheet music or whiteboard overlay where relevant (per [[feedback-pb-script-write-v2-lessons]] "whiteboard once max")

Specify these in the hook brief's "Visual plan" line per beat.
