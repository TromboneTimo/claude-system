# Hook opener patterns (4 archetypes), when to pick which

From the 40-video Antoine corpus sweep, the strongest hooks (44-46 / 60) used one of 4 opener archetypes. The weakest (28-32) all opened with "Good morning." Pick the archetype based on the idea's emotional register, then clone the rhythm.

## Pattern 1. Stat-shock + immediate reframe

Scored 43-44 in the corpus. Examples: KBT0roUguhc (44), sLpOh6wpQZs (43).

Shape (in order):
1. **Stat-shock sentence**: a hard percentage about what most people in the audience do wrong. "95% of adult beginners practice way too much and they still can't play the right note." "90% of beginners think they have to practice for hours."
2. **One-line reframe**: a "But what if..." or "But the truth is..." sentence that flips the failed effort into a permission to do less or differently. "But what if 15 minutes a day were enough?"
3. **Authority anchor**: "After playing X for Y years and teaching N students, I found..."
4. **Named method + promise**: ties the authority to a brandable framework with a time-bound outcome.

**Use when:** the idea's pain point is "I've been doing X harder for years and it isn't working" (effort-guilt). Best fit for Harrisson topics like "you've been told to use more air for 20 years and your range hasn't moved", "stop grinding long tones", "stop blowing harder."

**Trumpet template:**
> "[N]% of trumpet players [specific failed behavior], and they still can't [specific outcome they want]. But what if the real fix is [reframe]? After teaching hundreds of comeback players, I found that [the named technique] is what unlocks it. So today I'll show you [3-beat preview]..."

## Pattern 2. Story cold-open

Scored 46 in the corpus. Example: J0OdaOyrS1c (46), wN7hzc-GabQ (38, lower because the story is weaker).

Shape:
1. **Narrative hook** (3-4 sentences): a vivid story about a known figure or a specific student. NOT about Harrisson. About someone the viewer is curious about. "When people heard Paganini play for the first time, many believed it was not humanly possible. Some thought he had made a deal with the devil."
2. **Truth-reveal pivot**: "But the truth behind X was much more interesting..."
3. **Authority anchor + named method**: "After reading 800 books and studying the practice habits of the best..."
4. **Structure preview**: "Today I'll show you..."

**Use when:** the idea has a great anchor story (a famous trumpet player's hidden technique, a real comeback student's transformation, a teacher's surprising confession). Best fit for Harrisson topics like "the embouchure secret pro trumpet players actually use", "how [legend] really got their range", "this student went from G to double C in 90 days."

**Trumpet template:**
> "[Famous trumpet player or real student] [played something impossible-seeming]. Most [trumpet community] thought it was [genetic gift / endless practice / impossible]. But the real reason was much simpler. After [authority anchor], I found [named method]. Today I'll show you [structure preview]..."

## Pattern 3. Empathy disarm

Scored 40 in the corpus. Example: Ox0V5_ZnF4c (40).

Shape:
1. **Empathy line**: "There is nothing wrong with you. [Hard thing] is hard. It was for me."
2. **Authority anchor**: same stack as the others.
3. **Named method + numbered rules**: "...I discovered three golden rules that allow hundred of my students to develop a perfect sound."
4. **Promise + structure preview**.

**Use when:** the idea's pain point is shame-based (self-blame, "I must be talentless", "I'm too old", "my chops are just wrong"). Best fit for Harrisson topics like "you're not too old", "your chops aren't broken", "comeback players over 50".

**Trumpet template:**
> "There is nothing wrong with you. [Specific shame-based pain] is hard. It was for me too. After [authority anchor], I discovered [N named rules / points / steps] that let hundreds of my students [outcome]. Today I'll show you [structure preview]..."

## Pattern 4. Diagnostic-question cold open

Scored 40 in the corpus. Examples: LfgFjHMzIus (40), kPz8vF6R-Ic (31, weaker because the diagnostic is buried).

Shape:
1. **Direct question**: matches the literal search query of the ICP. "Can you still learn to play the violin beautifully as an adult beginner?"
2. **Counter the prevailing wisdom**: "Like a lot of [authority figures] says, [X is true]. But over the last 10 years..."
3. **Authority + named method**: "...I teach hundreds of [specific subgroup], some of them [extreme case], and what I found was [named insight]."
4. **Tease the body**: a story or framework preview.

**Use when:** the idea's title IS a high-search-volume question. Best fit for Harrisson topics that target a specific objection or demographic: "Can you build range after 50?", "Should you switch mouthpieces?", "Is your high school band director's embouchure advice killing your range?"

**Trumpet template:**
> "Can you still [specific outcome] as a [specific subgroup]? Or is [the discouraging conventional wisdom] true? Over the last [N] years, I've taught hundreds of [exact subgroup], including [extreme case]. Today I'll share [a true story] and [the N things every successful student did]..."

## ANTI-PATTERN: "Good morning" + soft opener

Scored 28-32 in the corpus. Examples: WB70U3RBuq0 (36, on the high end for this pattern), rH4QbHB8uIA (32), KhBZfzSvrJg (30), _BDrcqm-9Ok (28).

Why it fails: the greeting eats valuable first-sentence real estate. The viewer's curiosity isn't earned by "Good morning. Today I'll show you..."

**Only use when:** every other pattern scores below 40 for THIS idea AND the topic genuinely requires a calm-teacher tone (rare). Default to one of the 4 winning patterns.

## Picking algorithm for pb-hook-design Step 1

Given an approved idea row, the 4 ranking agents each score the entire 40-video corpus on the rubric. Tag each Antoine video with its archetype from above. The aggregated top 3 will surface 1-3 archetypes. Present the top 3 hooks to Timo, each labeled by archetype. Timo picks. If two hooks tie on score, prefer the one whose archetype matches the idea's emotional register (see "Use when" notes above).
