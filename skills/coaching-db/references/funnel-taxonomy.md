# Funnel Taxonomy: TOFU / MOFU / BOFU

Every quote gets tagged with exactly one funnel stage. This reference defines the boundaries.

## TOFU (Top of Funnel). Pain recognition.

The speaker is describing a problem. They have not yet connected the problem to a solution. The language centers on frustration with status quo or a specific struggle moment.

**Tag TOFU when the quote:**
- Names a pain point ("my range is shot")
- Describes a struggle moment ("I tried to play a scale and the tone went off around D")
- Expresses identity loss or frustration without a resolution
- Describes the BEFORE state
- Says what was hard, not what worked

**Examples:**
- "I was struggling with attacks. I was struggling with pianissimo playing."
- "I've been playing 30 years and my range is worse than college."
- "By the end of it all, I was exhausted and I ended up using pressure because I just couldn't do it."

**Use for:** cold ad copy, YouTube hooks targeting an unsolved pain, SEO content that names the problem, email subject lines that echo the prospect's own frustration.

## MOFU (Middle of Funnel). Consideration.

The speaker is aware of possible solutions. They are evaluating options, naming what they tried that failed, or describing their decision journey. The language centers on comparison, research, and the narrowing from "there must be an answer" to "this might be it."

**Tag MOFU when the quote:**
- Lists what the speaker tried before ("I did the Stamp, the Clarke, the Schlossberg")
- Describes the gap between prior methods and desired outcome
- Names the decision criteria
- Explains why they chose this solution over another
- Surfaces an objection the speaker overcame

**Examples:**
- "I tried every method book. Nothing stuck."
- "I wasn't sure if another program would actually be different."
- "I needed accountability, not just more exercises."

**Use for:** retargeting ads that address objections, comparison content, email sequences that position Harrison vs generic methods, sales page body copy.

## BOFU (Bottom of Funnel). Outcome and decision.

The speaker is describing a specific outcome, transformation, or endorsement. The language centers on before/after with numbers, timeframes, or unique proof. Post-purchase testimonial language lives here.

**Tag BOFU when the quote:**
- Names a specific result ("I gained an octave in 30 days")
- Describes the AFTER state with detail
- Recommends the program in testimonial language
- Describes identity shift ("I am the player I was at 25")
- Names a moment of breakthrough with context

**Examples:**
- "Some notes I didn't even know I could play and they were popping out."
- "In three days I got a high C consistent. I had not played a high C in 8 years."
- "Best trumpet teachers I have had."

**Use for:** social proof blocks, testimonial carousels, BOFU email closers, landing page headlines backed by specifics, case studies.

## Edge cases and rulings

| Situation | Ruling |
|---|---|
| Quote has both pain and outcome in one sentence | Split if possible. Otherwise tag the dominant half. If truly balanced, tag MOFU. |
| Quote is a recommendation but names a pain | BOFU if the recommendation is the center. TOFU if the pain is the center. |
| Quote is pre-purchase optimism ("I think this will work") | MOFU. It is the decision journey mid-stream. |
| Quote is a commenter asking a question ("Can you do a video on X?") | TOFU. Represents unsolved pain at the awareness stage. |
| Quote is a prospect on a sales call naming what they tried | MOFU. Consideration in progress. |
| Quote is a customer on a testimonial video reflecting on before-state | TOFU. Even though the speaker is post-purchase, the language itself is TOFU and works in TOFU copy. |

The last rule matters. Speaker status and quote funnel stage are independent. A customer retrospectively describing frustration is still gold for TOFU copy.
