Blog posts must NOT be written as direct product-vs-product comparisons that read like ads for Robinson's Remedies.

Instead, use a listicle format ("5 Best X for Y") that:
- Covers multiple real options/solutions to the reader's problem
- Includes competitors, prescriptions, supplements, and other approaches honestly
- Has pros AND cons for every item, including RR's product
- Positions RR as #1/Best Overall with a "Top Pick" badge, but earns it through transparent methodology
- Includes a disclosure like "Robinson's Remedies is our product" for trust
- Lets the comparison table and facts do the convincing rather than marketing copy

**Why:** User explicitly flagged the direct comparison approach as looking like an ad, not content. The listicle format builds trust with readers and performs better for SEO (targets broader "best X" keywords).

**How to apply:** Every blog post about products should review the full landscape of solutions. RR earns the top spot through ingredient count, dual function (treat + prevent), and wax-free absorption, not through biased copy.
