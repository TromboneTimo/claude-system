From rebuilding the "Hannah / How many years" email through ~10 rounds of Timo corrections. Canonical detail + the centered-video HTML + the metrics script live in `~/.claude/skills/pb-email-write/references/ed-style-and-revision-lessons.md` (now loaded by pb-email-write). Highlights:

**Why these came up:** I drafted before fully reading the source, softballed the transformation, overstated a testimonial claim past the witness's words, and kept answering near-versions of Timo's exact asks. Each cost a round.

**How to apply:**
- **Read the FULL transcript/testimonial before writing one word about a student.** Hannah's real wins (lead playing, improv, music school) were in the part I had not read. Don't draft off a truncated pull.
- **Stay inside the witness's exact words.** "Confidence to go after lead" is not "is gigging lead." Write "going after," not the harder fact, unless Timo confirms it from outside the video. Overstating a testimonial blocks the ship.
- **The link is the source of truth for who the student is** (Joinville template -> the link was Mike, then Hannah). Resolve via oEmbed first. See [[feedback_master_lessons]] (unusual name = verify).
- **Default structure = Ed Lawrence:** short question hook -> "That was me" confession -> woven reframe -> proof student with quote-alone-at-the-pivot -> centered video -> P.S. carries the offer. Timo loves the question open and the natural P.S.-as-offer.
- **Answer the literal ask.** "Show full emails" = full, not quotes. "Reform the question" = questions, not statements.
- **Run the metrics pass EARLY** (word count vs corpus, readability, I:you ratio, paragraph walls) to settle taste with data instead of five rounds of vibes.
- Calibrate harder/more emotionally specific for Harrison's list; my safe default read as "soft" to Timo repeatedly. Synthesize toward the middle, don't swing to choppy. See [[feedback_email_friend_to_friend_depth]].
