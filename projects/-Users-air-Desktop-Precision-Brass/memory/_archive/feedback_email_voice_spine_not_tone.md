2026-06-01: Timo, after weeks of back-and-forth tuning email TONE (friendly vs naggy vs whimsical), said the drafts still felt "soft-cocked." Diagnosis, backed by research (`~/.claude/research/perplexity/raw/2026-05-31_daily-email-vs-weekly-email...`): the problem was never tone. It was SPINE. The drafts were value-only / story-only with a limp CTA ("come hang out and I'll walk you through it"), which research shows trains readers to consume, not buy.

**Why:** We kept treating "whimsical friend voice" (Ed Lawrence / Dimitri, which Timo loves) and "actually sell" as a trade-off. They are NOT. Every master the research names (Ben Settle, Ian Stanley, André Chaperon, Justin Goff) AND Ed Lawrence + Dimitri all do BOTH at once: entertaining story-driven delivery on top, one clear commercial pivot underneath. Picking only the friendly half is what produced the soft husks Timo hated. Value-only daily emails underperform on revenue because: they train consumption not buying, give no trackable buying pathway, and cause invisible engagement churn.

**The locked voice spec for every pb-email / pb-email-write draft (broadcast/daily):**
1. **Cold open straight into a scene.** No windup ("over the last 10 years..."). Open mid-event, like Dimitri or the Dave Richards email: "A couple years back I played some gigs with Dave Richards." (See [[feedback_no_invented_story_details]], real verified facts only.)
2. **One story, one idea, one emotional angle.** Not three lessons. Over-talking IS the bug. Match the swipe-source economy, not just its skeleton ([[feedback_email_friend_to_friend_depth]] still holds for DEPTH/teaching, but depth does not mean word-count bloat).
3. **The story is the VEHICLE to the offer, not decoration.** The pivot from story to ask must be tight, the way Dimitri pivots "he joined, I spotted it in 10 seconds" straight into "the camp is built for that."
4. **Every email has ONE explicit, named ask.** Soft-sell is fine. NO-sell is banned. Replace limp "come hang out" with a specific reason to act NOW. Soft-sell still bridges to a commercial next step.
5. **Rotate the ask across the batch (Timo's call 2026-06-01):** some go to free masterclass (training-room URL), some to reply-to-me (engagement + warm-lead surfacing, very Ed Lawrence), some to book a call / application. Aim for variety across a batch; pick the ask the story naturally sets up. Always exactly ONE ask per email.
6. **Whimsy/personality lives in delivery + P.S.**, spine lives in structure. Keep Harrisson likable (self-deprecating, real stories, contractions, direct 2nd-person address) AND make every send move the reader toward buying.

**Meta-lesson for me:** stop oscillating on tone when Timo says "soft." Soft = no spine = add a real ask, do not add more friendliness. And do the research FIRST (he was right to be annoyed I had not). Run `/research check` before drafting a voice; the answers were already in the cache from the day before.
