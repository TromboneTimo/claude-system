# Never Fabricate Specifics When Generating Content

**Rule:** Every dollar figure, engagement number, client metric, or specific fact in generated content must be traceable to Timo's actual database or explicitly marked as hypothetical/placeholder. No inventing "$4,200 per Instagram post" or "$6K agency" or "347K followers" for narrative convenience.

**Why:** On 2026-04-12 I generated Power Content ideas that invented specific numbers (agency cost per post, follower counts, earnings) to make the hooks sound concrete. Timo caught it immediately: "you are hallucinating business numbers, nothing you come up with is rooted in reality." This is the same failure pattern as the Precision Brass fabrication -- narrative pressure makes invented specifics feel like good writing when they're actually lies. The master lesson is already in memory (feedback_fabricated_behavior.md) but I violated it anyway because the multi-persona skill REWARDED specificity without requiring provenance.

**How to apply:**
- Before writing any hook or body with a number in it: check if that number exists in Timo's database. If not, ASK or use a generic placeholder.
- Real proof arsenal (verified, usable): 1B+ views across platforms, 1M+ followers, $40K from one 60-second YouTube video, $2K from one TikTok, 710K views in 12 hours, $8.5K/mo AdSense. Harrison Ball $50K/mo, 100% close rate, 12-30 organic leads/day. Robinson's Remedies tripled sales. Dallas Symphony 500% case study. Victor 30K views first video, $3K coaching program. Steve Parker 500K views, sold out 5 exhibitions. Big Wy's 5M views, Applebee's brand deal. Norfolk 10K YT followers from old footage. Third Coast 1M+ views. NEVER go beyond these without verification.
- When an idea "needs" a specific number that doesn't exist in the database: either cut the specificity, use Timo's verified proof, or ASK him for the real number.
- Multi-persona generation systems that reward creative specificity without provenance checks are STRUCTURALLY dangerous. Don't rebuild them. Always ground in real artifacts first.
- If Timo hasn't stated his offer fully, ASK before writing marketing for it. His actual offer (as of 2026-04-12): organic content marketing structure + AI systems for websites, newsletters, dashboards, ads -- plus coaching, plus the library. Don't narrow him to one thing.

**Invitation != achievement (2026-04-13 follow-up).** If the database says "invited to X" or "offered Y," I must NOT present it as a credential meaning "did X" or "achieved Y." Timo's TED talk was an invitation that was never delivered. Treating it as a credential is the same failure mode as fabricating numbers -- inflating a real fact past what it actually says. When in doubt, strip the invitation credential entirely. Use only things he actually DID.
