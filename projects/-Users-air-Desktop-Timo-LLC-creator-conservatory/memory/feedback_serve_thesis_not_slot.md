# Serve the Thesis, Don't Fill the Slot

**Rule:** When a slide or section has a placeholder (quote, image, stat, example), the question is NEVER "what can I put here that's famous/verified/related?" The question is "does this element directly serve the thesis of the surrounding slides?" If yes, keep. If no, cut or replace with a direct statement in Timo's voice.

**Why:** On 2026-04-13, Timo asked for a quote that said "test organically first, then pay." I failed 5 times in a row:
- First filled with Ogilvy ("Never stop testing") — about testing generally, not organic-first
- Then swapped to Wanamaker ("Half the money I spend is wasted") — about measurement/waste, not organic-first
- Each swap I thought I was fixing it because the quote was famous and verified

Root cause pattern: I treated "quote slide" as a slot to fill rather than a beat in an argument. I prioritized:
- Famous (recognizability) OVER on-thesis (meaning)
- Verified (no fabrication) OVER aligned (serves argument)
- Filling the slot OVER cutting or rewriting

## The Right Question Sequence

For every placeholder element before shipping:

1. **What is the thesis of the surrounding 2-3 slides?** (State in one sentence.)
2. **Does this element directly reinforce that thesis?** (Yes / No / Tangentially.)
3. **If tangentially:** Can I find something that DIRECTLY reinforces? If not, would the deck be stronger WITHOUT this element?
4. **If no direct verified source exists:** Write the principle in Timo's voice on a bold text slide. Don't fake attribution.

## Don't Force a Slot

Empty slots are NOT defects. A tight 13-slide deck with every beat earning its place beats a 15-slide deck with 2 filler quotes that break flow. The correct answer to "can I find a quote for this slide?" is sometimes "no, cut the slide."

## Fake Authority is Worse Than No Authority

If I attribute a paraphrased principle to a real person as a verbatim quote, that's fabrication even if the person has said similar things. Invented verbatim + real name = lie. Better paths:
- Cut the slide entirely
- State the principle directly in Timo's voice (no attribution)
- Use a "Based on [person]'s framework" credit WITHOUT putting quotation marks around paraphrased content
