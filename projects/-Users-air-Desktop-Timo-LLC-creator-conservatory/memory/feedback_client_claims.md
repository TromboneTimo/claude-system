**NEVER use specific client names or specific dollar figures in any public-facing content** (ads, presentations, social posts, emails to prospects, landing pages, etc.).

## BANNED phrasings in public content
- "Harrison makes $50K/month because of me"
- "Harrison: $50K/mo, 100% close rate"
- "I tripled Robinson's Remedies sales"
- "Robinson's Remedies: 3x growth"
- Any specific client name + specific result

## APPROVED phrasings for the same achievements
Instead of naming clients or exact dollars, use:
- "My organic systems powered by AI have brought high-ticket sales worth thousands of dollars to independent coaches and creators."
- "I've driven record-breaking sales and growth for ecommerce companies in the music space."
- "Clients using my systems have closed five-figure deals."
- "I've helped small music brands achieve multiples of their previous revenue baselines."
- "My AI systems power marketing for clients generating thousands per month in high-ticket sales."

**Why:** Specific client names and dollar amounts in public content risk client privacy, client relationships, and contractual obligations. Timo has NDAs, informal trust, and a brand built on discretion with his roster. Naming names in an ad is how you lose the trust of everyone else paying you.

**How to apply:**
- In ads, slides, presentations, Facebook posts, YouTube videos, blog posts, landing pages: anonymize everything
- In private DMs after someone commented a value bomb keyword: you CAN be more specific IF asked for case studies, because it's 1-to-1 not 1-to-many
- In proposals to THE CLIENT themselves (like the RR dashboard proposal): specific numbers are fine because it's between you and them
- In internal planning documents (like the skill files): specific figures are fine because these never go public

## Credentials to lead with instead (verifiable, public, impressive)

**Performance credits (LEAD WITH THESE):**
- Performed with The Temptations
- Performed with The O'Jays
- Performed with The Four Tops
- Performed with Richmond Symphony
- Performed with Chicago Symphony

**Education (second tier):**
- Yale
- Northwestern
- UT Austin

**Viewership proof (third tier):**
- Over 1 billion views across platforms
- Over 1 million followers
- NBC News feature
- TED talk invitation

**Order to present on slides:** Performance credits > Universities > Viewership numbers > Anonymized client wins

Do NOT flip the order. The performance credits are the most unique and hardest to fake, which makes them the best lead.
