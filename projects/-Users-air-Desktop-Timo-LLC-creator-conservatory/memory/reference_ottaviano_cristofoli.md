## Ottaviano Cristofoli ("Otto")

**Born:** August 29, 1986, Udine, Italy (Friuli Venezia Giulia, near Friulan Dolomites)
**Current residence:** Tokyo, Japan (since 2008)

### Current Positions
- **Solo (Principal) Trumpet, Japan Philharmonic Orchestra** (since 2009)
- **Artistic Director, Music System Italy/Japan** (co-founded 2015, AD since 2019)
- **Member, Samurai BRASS** (since 2016) - elite ensemble of Japan's top brass players

### Education
- Started saxophone age 6, switched to trumpet age 10 under Prof. Fabiano Cudiz
- Graduated Conservatory of Udine "J. Tomadini" in 2004 (age 17) under Prof. Marco Tampieri
- Further study with Prof. Davide Simoncini
- Private study in Chicago with CSO members: Dale Clevenger (horn), Tage Larsen, Chris Martin, Rex Martin (trumpets)

### Career Timeline
- **2004-2006:** Italian Youth Orchestra (Music School of Fiesole)
- **2006-2008:** Schleswig-Holstein Musik Festival Orchestra (Germany) under Christoph Eschenbach
- **2004-2008:** Principal/first trumpet with major Italian orchestras: La Verdi (Milan), Teatro Carlo Felice (Genoa), Teatro San Carlo (Naples), La Fenice (Venice), Haydn Orchestra (Bolzano/Trento), Teatro Verdi (Trieste), Teatro dell'Opera di Roma, Accademia Nazionale di Santa Cecilia
- **2007:** Associate Member of Chicago Civic Orchestra and Miami New World Symphony; qualified as substitute principal trumpet for Chicago Symphony Orchestra
- **2008:** Won audition for co-principal trumpet of Hyogo Performing Arts Center Orchestra (Kobe) under Yutaka Sado
- **2009-present:** Principal trumpet, Japan Philharmonic Orchestra (offered position by artistic director Alexander Lazarev)
- Guest principal with Seoul Philharmonic and Philadelphia Orchestra

### Notable Performances
- **2015:** World premiere of *Tokyo Suite* (Concerto for Trumpet and Orchestra by Gabriele Roberto), dedicated to him, commissioned by JPO
- **December 2021:** Arutunian Trumpet Concerto + Mahler 5 at Suntory Hall with Kahchun Wong (sold out)

### Discography
1. **Fulgor (Inazuma)** (2015/2016) - Solo debut, 15 tracks of pieces dedicated to him
2. **Azura: The Italian Recital** (2018) - With pianist Doremi Takahashi and trombonist Eijiro Nakagawa
3. **Romeo and Juliet** (2022) - Music System Japan Brass Ensemble, 16 tracks
4. **Seasons** (2023) - Reinvention of Vivaldi's Four Seasons, arranged by Mario Corvini for trumpet
- Also records film/TV/commercial/radio soundtracks
