Every piece of short-form persuasion content for Timo (sales deck, reel script, carousel, YouTube short, email, VSL) must pass this 6-point audit before ship. Not optional. Not "most of them." All 6.

**Why:** 2026-04-13 audit of 4 decks (Leaking Business, AI Music Marketing, Views That Matter, Designer vs Marketer). Scores: 5/6, 4.5/6, 3.5/6, 3/6. The **same failure modes appeared across all 4 decks** — not random mistakes, a systematic pattern. Root cause: I was thesis-first (what I want to argue) instead of viewer-first (what the viewer needs to keep watching and act). That's a structural blindspot I will fix by encoding this gate.

## THE 6-POINT GATE

| # | Check | What PASS looks like | What FAIL looks like |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hook — ceiling-client-specific** | Names **who + situation + pain** in one sentence. "Music coaches spending $2K/mo on Facebook ads that aren't converting..." | Generic category label. "Hey musicians!" or "For music coaches." Missing the priced situation. |
| 2 | **Promise of value** | Explicit time commitment + specific payoff. "Give me the next 3 minutes and I'll show you..." | Implicit, missing, or vague ("Let me share some thoughts"). |
| 3 | **Early CTA / Attention anchor** | Within the first third, a "stick around / keep watching / you'll want to see this next" moment that creates a mid-scroll reason to stay. | Hook → argument → close with nothing in the middle to hold attention. 4 of 4 decks failed this on 2026-04-13. This is my #1 instinct gap. |
| 4 | **Humble brag / credibility** | Quick, specific proof. "1.5B views, performed with The Temptations, tripled a client's sales." Not a flex reel. | Either missing entirely or a 4-slide resume recital. |
| 5 | **Meat and potatoes — ONE thing** | ONE concrete actionable takeaway the viewer could apply TODAY. | Three frameworks, a 5-point system, a grid of "quick wins." If you can't say "the one thing they should do is X" in 12 words, it's too many things. |
| 6 | **Closing CTA with NAMED value bomb** | Specific trigger word + **specific deliverable + the benefit of that deliverable**. "Comment LEAK. I'll send you the 3-point content audit I run before I look at anybody's ad spend." | Trigger word + vague deliverable. "Comment REBUILD, I'll show you what your site should have been" fails because it doesn't name what the viewer actually gets or why they'd want it. |

## WHEN TO RUN THE GATE

Before declaring done on ANY of these artifacts for Timo:
- HTML presentation / slide deck
- VSL script
- YouTube or TikTok short script
- Carousel or reel copy
- Email (especially first-send to cold list)
- Sales page headline + above-fold
- Paid ad copy

## THE PRE-SHIP RITUAL (3 steps, ~90 seconds)

1. **Read the artifact from the viewer's perspective**, muted if visual, with no prior context. Write a one-line score for each of the 6 items: PASS / PARTIAL / FAIL.
2. **Any FAIL or PARTIAL = fix before ship**, not ship-and-iterate. The cost of shipping a 3/6 deck is wasted audience attention; the cost of one more fix pass is 10 minutes.
3. **If hook scored PARTIAL**, run the three-part test: does the hook name **WHO** + **SITUATION** + **PAIN** in one sentence? If any of those three is missing, rewrite. Generate 3 variants, pick the most specific.

## EARLY CTA INSERTS (copy-paste-adapt)

For the Early CTA in particular, since I miss it instinctively, here are drop-ins to use after the setup but before the main argument lands:

- "Stick around — the fix isn't what you think."
- "Keep watching — this next part is where most [ICP] get it wrong."
- "Stay with me — I'm about to show you a pattern you've been blind to."
- "Hang tight — the fix is counterintuitive and works for [range of audience sizes]."

## URGENCY / WARNING LABEL PATTERN (approved by Timo 2026-04-13)

For AI-era content specifically, add a slide 2-3 warning that primes tension:

- "WARNING: What you're about to see wasn't possible 12 months ago. AI is improving so fast your competition is using tools your agency hasn't heard of. Every month you wait, the gap widens."
- Variant: "WARNING: If you're running 2024 playbooks, your competitors have already lapped you twice. This lesson expires soon."

Use the `.urgency-slide` class pattern (red border/accent, short, punchy, high-contrast type).

## EXAMPLES FROM THE 2026-04-13 AUDIT

**Hook specificity — BEFORE vs AFTER:**
- BEFORE (Designer deck): "For music coaches & business owners" → abstract category
- AFTER: "Music coaches spending $5K on websites that convert under 2% — your designer just cost you $50K in lost sign-ups." → names WHO, SITUATION, PAIN

**Close CTA — BEFORE vs AFTER:**
- BEFORE (Designer deck): "Comment REBUILD, I'll show you what your site should have been" → vague deliverable, no benefit
- AFTER: "Comment REBUILD. I'll send you a 3-point audit showing the one headline change that would triple your conversions." → specific deliverable + named benefit

## THE META-RULE

When generating Timo content, don't start from "what do I want to argue." Start from "what does the viewer need in order to (a) stop scrolling, (b) stay watching, and (c) take the action at the end." The 6-point gate IS that viewer-first scaffold. Use it as a writing template, not just an audit.
