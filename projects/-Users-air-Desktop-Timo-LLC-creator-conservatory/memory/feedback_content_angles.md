When Timo shows multiple content angle options, do NOT immediately rank them. First audit the frame.

**Why:** 2026-04-13 Timo showed 5 angles for Amy Portie FB ads system. All 5 were tool-focused (AI/Claude Code as hero). I picked favorites instead of noticing they all shared the same weak frame. Only when Timo offered "designers aren't marketers" did I realize the stronger lane existed. He asked why I didn't catch it first.

**How to apply (in order):**

1. **Same lane test** - Are all the options sharing the same underlying frame? If yes, ask "what frame is missing?" not "which option wins?"

2. **Problem-first vs tool-first** - Default to problem-focused angles. "Your $3K website isn't converting" beats "I built this in 40 minutes with AI." Audience cares about their pain, not your capability. Tool is HOW you solve, not WHY they listen.

3. **Enemy test** - Every strong angle has a clear enemy. "Designers aren't marketers" = enemy is bad designers. "AI is faster than ChatGPT" = no enemy, just a feature comparison. No enemy = weak angle.

4. **Timo's identity is MARKETER who uses AI, not AI guy.** Billion views, Harrison $50K/mo, Robinson's tripled are marketing stories not AI stories. Lead with marketing identity. AI is tool, not identity. "AI guy" positioning = every other grifter on Twitter.

5. **Pressure-test, don't admire.** When evaluating his work, job is to find what's MISSING, not what's good. "These are solid" is the wrong answer. "All five are in the same lane, here's the missing lane" is the right answer.

**Trigger:** Any time Timo shows content angles, hooks, marketing ideas, or asks for content suggestions. Run the 5 checks before responding.
