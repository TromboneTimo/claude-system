When describing a client deliverable in a proposal, contract, email, or any client-facing copy, do NOT label it "AI-generated," "AI-written," "AI-summarized," or name the underlying tool (Fireflies, ChatGPT, Claude, Otter, etc.). The client is paying for the deliverable. They do not need to know how the sausage is made.

Acceptable: "written summary," "recording," "session notes," "transcript."
Banned: "AI-generated summary," "AI-written notes," "captured by Fireflies AI agent," "Fireflies AI summary."

**Why:** 2026-05-09 IPO proposal. Timo said he'd record via Fireflies and provide a recording + summary. I wrote "AI-generated written summary" and "Fireflies AI agent" into the contract. He pushed back: "Don't say it's written by AI. Just say they'll get a summary. Come on." Naming the tool/method makes the deliverable feel cheap and undermines the $500 perceived value. The orchestra is buying a summary, not buying "the output of an AI tool."

**How to apply:**
- In proposals: describe the deliverable in plain client-value terms ("written summary of key points and action items"). Internal tooling stays internal.
- Same rule applies to research, transcripts, blog drafts, social copy, anything ending up in a client doc.
- If the user explicitly says "tell them it's AI-generated" then comply. Otherwise default to plain naming.
- Generalizes the anti-guru / anti-bro-marketer voice rule. The tool used is not the deliverable; the deliverable is the deliverable.
