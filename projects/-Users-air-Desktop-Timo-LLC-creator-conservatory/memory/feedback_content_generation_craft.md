# What Actually Moves Power Content Scores Up

From the 2026-04-13 session where 3 of 4 angles were lifted from 28-31 to 35-36 / 40. These patterns caused the jumps.

## The 5 Patterns That Move Scores

**1. Specific situations > generic complaints.**
- "You've fired three marketing agencies in two years" (specific pattern the ICP is IN)
- NOT "your agency is ripping you off" (generic complaint)
- Pattern: put the viewer inside an ongoing behavior loop, not outside pointing at a category.

**2. Stack 3+ proof points per body.**
- One proof is a claim. Three is a pattern.
- AI Lovers angle jumped 7 points when I named three specific shipped systems instead of one.
- Business Owners jumped 6 points adding legacy orchestra + retreat + Trombone Timo AdSense.

**3. Apply Portie principles, don't quote them.**
- "Portie's rule applies HERE because..." beats "as Portie said..."
- The quote is background. The APPLICATION is the insight.
- Every Portie cite should arrive as a knockout punch AFTER the setup, not as an authority-name-drop.

**4. Name the enemy's specific BEHAVIOR, not category.**
- "Free content identity" > "free content creators"
- "AI tourist collecting tools" > "AI enthusiasts"
- "Middleman agency with 40% margin" > "marketing agencies"
- Behavior-level attack causes self-identification. Category-level attack lets people dodge.

**5. Attack needs an exit door (permission/reframe).**
- Views That Matter jumped partly because it ended with "your audience is waiting for you to charge -- they're not going to revolt, they're going to buy."
- Attack without reframe = defensiveness.
- Attack + permission = conversion.

## My Generation Process Going Forward

**Before writing any Power Content / ad creative:**
1. Read source files from disk in the SAME turn (not from memory). TIMO_PROFILE.md, audience.md, context/LAUREL_PORTIE_DATABASE.md, any relevant transcripts.
2. Name the enemy in 3 words MAX before writing the hook. If I can't, the hook will be mushy.
3. Identify 3+ proof points from the database before drafting. If fewer than 3, the idea is too weak.
4. Pick the Portie principle it maps to BEFORE writing. The principle shapes the body.
5. Write with inline source tags `(source)` on every claim. If untagged, cut it.

**Template structure for any angle:**
- Enemy name (3 words)
- Portie principle
- Specific ongoing situation the ICP is in
- 3 stacked proof points
- Permission/reframe exit
- CTA keyword

**Hard rules for every generation:**
- No client names in public copy. Describe situationally.
- No fabricated numbers. Every dollar, percentage, follower count traces to database.
- No multi-persona agent systems. They reward fabricated specificity. Generate directly.
- Attack voice, not corporate. No hedging, no em dashes.
- For presentation/slide prompts, put "no client names" and "no fabricated numbers" INSIDE the prompt as hard rules.
