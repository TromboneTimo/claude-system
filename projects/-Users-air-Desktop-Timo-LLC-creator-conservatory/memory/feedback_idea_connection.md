When building a deck, every slide must logically follow from the prior one. The transition word ("And", "But", "So", "Meanwhile", "Because") needs a real antecedent in the previous slide.

**Why:** 2026-04-13 leaking-business deck — I wrote slide 6 as "real buyers Google you" jumping straight from slide 5's Portie quote about Meta targeting. No bridge. Timo had to point out the disconnect: "slide 6 doesnt connect to slide 5 logic at all, why the fuck not?" The fix was to bridge: "If content IS the targeting (slide 5), then what about the buyer who never sees your ad and Googles you instead?" That makes slide 5 the *premise* slide 6 builds on.

**How to apply:**
- After every slide edit, read the deck top-to-bottom AS A CONTINUOUS SCRIPT, not slide-by-slide.
- For each transition word/connector at the start of a slide, find the antecedent in the prior slide. If you can't find it, the bridge is broken.
- If two slides cover the same beat with different framings, one of them should be cut.
- Map the deck spine before writing copy: hook → insight → insight → quote (lands the insight) → next insight (builds on the quote) → diagnosis → solution → CTA. Each beat earns the next.
- "Adjacent slides need a logical handoff" applies even when the deck is mostly typographic. The eye can jump; the argument can't.
